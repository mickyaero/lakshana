ATA Chapter:         24 — Electrical Power

SUBJECT

Brake Control Unit Software Update

APPLICABILITY

Aircraft Type:       Embraer E190
Serial Numbers:      MSN 1817 through MSN 1887
Engine(s):           CF34-10E
Effectivity:         All aircraft within the serial range listed above unless previously
                     modified per SB-EMB-24-256 Rev. 02.

REASON FOR ISSUE

This Service Bulletin is issued to address FAA reported potential anti-skid logic discrepancy under wet runway conditions. Operator reports and in-service
investigations indicate that continued operation without the corrective action described
herein may result in degraded system performance and increased maintenance burden.

BACKGROUND AND RISK ASSESSMENT

A recent in-service occurrence on an operator in the Asia-Pacific region prompted a fleet-wide review of the component history. The investigation determined that aircraft operating in high-humidity, high-cycle environments are statistically more likely to exhibit the wear pattern described in section 3. While no immediate corrective action is mandated, the manufacturer strongly recommends compliance within the timeframe specified to maintain expected component reliability and dispatch availability.

Residual risk after compliance: NEGLIGIBLE.

DESCRIPTION OF CHANGE

Update BCU software from v2.18 to v2.20 per maintenance procedure 32-44-10.

COMPLIANCE

Recommended compliance: Within 6,000 flight hours
or 6 months from the date of this Service Bulletin,
whichever occurs first.

ESTIMATED LABOR

Manhours:            9 MH per aircraft (two technicians, station-level)
Estimated Material Cost: USD 4,069
Special Tooling:     None / Standard line tooling
